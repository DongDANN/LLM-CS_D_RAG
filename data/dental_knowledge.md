# Dental Clinic Knowledge Base

## Services Offered

### Dental Cleaning (Prophylaxis)
Professional teeth cleaning removes plaque and tartar buildup that regular brushing cannot reach. Recommended every 6 months. The procedure takes 30-45 minutes and includes scaling, polishing, and fluoride treatment. Price range: ₱500 - ₱1,500.

### Tooth Extraction (Simple)
Simple extraction is performed on visible teeth that are decayed, damaged, or causing crowding. The procedure uses local anesthesia and takes 20-30 minutes. Price range: ₱800 - ₱2,500.

### Tooth Extraction (Surgical/Wisdom Tooth)
Surgical extraction is required for impacted wisdom teeth or teeth broken below the gum line. This involves an incision and sometimes bone removal. Procedure time: 45-90 minutes. Price range: ₱3,000 - ₱8,000.

### Root Canal Treatment
Root canal therapy saves a severely infected or decayed tooth by removing the infected pulp, cleaning the canal, and sealing it. Usually requires 2-3 visits. Price range: ₱5,000 - ₱15,000 depending on the tooth.

### Dental Fillings
Fillings restore teeth damaged by cavities. We offer composite (tooth-colored) and amalgam (silver) fillings. Composite fillings: ₱800 - ₱2,500. Amalgam fillings: ₱500 - ₱1,500.

### Teeth Whitening
Professional teeth whitening can lighten teeth by several shades in one session. In-office whitening takes about 1 hour. Take-home whitening kits are also available. In-office: ₱5,000 - ₱15,000. Take-home kit: ₱3,000 - ₱5,000.

### Dental Braces (Orthodontics)
Braces correct misaligned teeth and bite issues. Treatment duration is typically 1-3 years. Options include metal braces, ceramic braces, and clear aligners. Metal braces: ₱30,000 - ₱60,000. Ceramic braces: ₱40,000 - ₱80,000. Clear aligners: ₱80,000 - ₱200,000.

### Dental Veneers
Veneers are thin shells placed over front teeth to improve appearance. They fix chips, stains, gaps, and minor misalignment. Porcelain veneers: ₱8,000 - ₱20,000 per tooth. Composite veneers: ₱3,000 - ₱8,000 per tooth.

### Dental Crown
A crown is a cap placed over a damaged tooth to restore its shape, size, and strength. Commonly needed after root canal treatment. Price range: ₱5,000 - ₱20,000 depending on material (porcelain, metal, or zirconia).

### Dental Bridge
A bridge replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring to adjacent teeth. It restores chewing function and prevents teeth from shifting. Price range: ₱15,000 - ₱45,000 depending on the number of units.

## Aftercare Instructions

### After Tooth Extraction
- Bite on the gauze pad for 30-45 minutes after the procedure.
- Do not spit, rinse, or use a straw for the first 24 hours.
- Apply an ice pack to reduce swelling (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off).
- Eat soft foods for 2-3 days (yogurt, soup, mashed potatoes).
- Avoid hot foods and drinks for 24 hours.
- Take prescribed pain medication as directed.
- Do not smoke for at least 72 hours.
- Gently rinse with warm salt water after 24 hours.

### After Root Canal
- Avoid chewing on the treated side until the permanent crown is placed.
- Some discomfort is normal for 2-3 days; take prescribed medication.
- Brush and floss normally, but be gentle around the treated area.
- Return for your follow-up appointment to complete the crown placement.
- Contact us if you experience severe pain, swelling, or fever.

### After Dental Filling
- Avoid eating until numbness wears off (1-2 hours) to prevent biting your cheek.
- Sensitivity to hot and cold is normal for a few days.
- Avoid very hard or sticky foods for 24 hours.
- If your bite feels uneven, contact us for an adjustment.

### After Teeth Whitening
- Avoid staining foods and drinks for 48 hours (coffee, tea, red wine, berries).
- Use a sensitivity toothpaste if you experience discomfort.
- Maintain results with good oral hygiene and touch-up treatments every 6-12 months.

## Emergency Dental Care

### When to Seek Emergency Care
- Severe toothache that doesn't respond to over-the-counter pain relief
- Knocked-out tooth (bring the tooth, keep it moist in milk)
- Broken or cracked tooth with sharp edges cutting your mouth
- Swelling in the face or gums with fever
- Uncontrolled bleeding after extraction

### First Aid for Dental Emergencies
- **Toothache:** Rinse with warm salt water, apply a cold compress, take ibuprofen.
- **Knocked-out tooth:** Handle by the crown (not the root), rinse gently, try to place back in socket or keep in milk, and get to a dentist within 30 minutes.
- **Broken tooth:** Rinse mouth, apply gauze to bleeding areas, use a cold compress, and visit us immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I visit the dentist?
We recommend a dental check-up and cleaning every 6 months. Patients with gum disease or other conditions may need more frequent visits.

### Does dental cleaning hurt?
Most patients experience little to no discomfort. If you have sensitive teeth or gum disease, we can apply a topical numbing gel before the procedure.

### How long do dental fillings last?
Composite fillings last 5-10 years. Amalgam fillings can last 10-15 years. Longevity depends on oral hygiene, diet, and the size of the filling.

### Are dental X-rays safe?
Yes. Modern digital X-rays use very low radiation (less than what you receive from a day of natural background radiation). We use lead aprons for additional protection.

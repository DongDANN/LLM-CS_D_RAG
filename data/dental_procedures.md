# Dental Procedures - Detailed Guide

## What to Expect During Common Procedures

### Dental Cleaning (Prophylaxis) - Step by Step
1. **Examination:** The dentist checks for cavities, gum disease, and oral health issues.
2. **Scaling:** A scaler or ultrasonic device removes plaque and tartar from teeth and along the gum line.
3. **Polishing:** A gritty toothpaste and electric brush polish teeth to remove surface stains.
4. **Flossing:** Professional flossing to clean between teeth.
5. **Fluoride treatment:** A fluoride gel or varnish is applied to strengthen enamel (optional).
6. **X-rays:** Taken once a year to detect hidden problems.

### Root Canal - Step by Step
1. **Diagnosis:** X-ray confirms infection has reached the tooth pulp.
2. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia numbs the area completely.
3. **Access:** A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth.
4. **Cleaning:** Infected pulp tissue is removed and canals are cleaned and shaped.
5. **Filling:** Canals are filled with gutta-percha (rubber-like material) and sealed.
6. **Crown:** A crown is placed to protect and restore the tooth (usually at a follow-up visit).

### Tooth Extraction - Step by Step
1. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia numbs the tooth and surrounding area.
2. **Loosening:** The dentist uses an elevator to loosen the tooth.
3. **Extraction:** Forceps are used to remove the tooth from the socket.
4. **Gauze:** A gauze pad is placed to control bleeding and help form a blood clot.
5. **Instructions:** Aftercare instructions are provided for healing.

### Getting Braces - Step by Step
1. **Consultation:** Assessment of bite, X-rays, photos, and impressions.
2. **Treatment plan:** Orthodontist explains the plan, timeline, and cost.
3. **Bonding:** Brackets are glued to each tooth, and a wire is threaded through.
4. **Adjustments:** Monthly visits to tighten wires and monitor progress.
5. **Removal:** Braces are removed when teeth are aligned (1-3 years).
6. **Retainer:** A retainer is provided to maintain the new position.

## Pain Management

### Before a Procedure
- Inform your dentist about any anxiety or past pain experiences.
- Ask about sedation options: nitrous oxide (laughing gas), oral sedation, or local anesthesia.
- Avoid caffeine before your appointment as it can increase anxiety.
- Get a good night's sleep before your procedure.

### During a Procedure
- You should feel pressure but NOT pain during any procedure.
- If you feel pain, raise your hand immediately — more anesthesia can be given.
- Nitrous oxide is available for anxious patients.

### After a Procedure
- Over-the-counter pain relief: Ibuprofen (Advil) or Paracetamol (Tylenol).
- Follow the prescribed medication schedule — don't wait for pain to start.
- Ice packs reduce swelling and numb the area.
- Soft foods and cold drinks help in the first 24-48 hours.
- Most post-procedure discomfort resolves within 3-5 days.

## When You Need a Crown vs. a Filling

### Choose a Filling When:
- The cavity is small to moderate.
- More than 50% of the tooth structure remains.
- The tooth is not a back molar bearing heavy chewing force.

### Choose a Crown When:
- The cavity is large (more than 50% of the tooth is affected).
- The tooth has had a root canal (now brittle and needs protection).
- The tooth is cracked or has large old fillings that are failing.
- You want to restore a badly worn or misshapen tooth.

## Dental Implants

### What Are Dental Implants?
Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed in the jawbone to replace missing tooth roots. A crown is then attached to the implant, creating a permanent replacement tooth that looks and functions like a natural tooth.

### Am I a Candidate?
- Adequate jawbone density (bone grafting is an option if not).
- Healthy gums without active periodontal disease.
- Non-smoker or willing to quit (smoking reduces success rate).
- Good overall health for surgery.

### Implant Timeline
1. **Consultation and planning:** X-rays, CT scan, treatment plan (1 visit).
2. **Implant placement:** Titanium post is placed in jawbone (1 visit, surgery).
3. **Healing:** 3-6 months for the implant to fuse with bone (osseointegration).
4. **Abutment:** A connector piece is placed on the implant (1 visit).
5. **Crown:** Custom crown is attached (1-2 visits).

### Implant Cost
- Single tooth implant: ₱50,000 - ₱120,000 (includes post, abutment, and crown).
- Cost depends on bone grafting needs, material choices, and complexity.
- More expensive upfront than bridges but lasts 20+ years with proper care.
